Meet the Development Team!

Jana Amin

Jana is Robin's project manager and full-stack engineer. She developed the bird detection algorithm, integrating voice commands for hands-free navigation, and collaborated with clients to facilitate team operations.

Meriam Harissa

Meriam is the lead researcher and full-stack engineer. She created a machine learning algorithm that improves bird prediction accuracy and used it to pinpoint best bird sighting locations based on the user’s current location.

Jodi Joven

Jodi is the lead UI/UX designer and full-stack engineer. She designed the entire user interface with an original design system and ensured the app's compatibility with screen readers on both iOS and Android for users requiring assistive tech.

Leah Mirch

Leah is Robin's scrum master and full-stack engineer. She developed the Robin chatbot, an interactive AI assistant that provides bird information, while also organizing and maintaining the project's backlog.

Special thanks to

Breanna Santos

Breanna is Robin's lead brand designer who created Robin’s logo. As an Illustration major at the College of Creative Studies in Detroit, she volunteered to establish a distinctive brand identity and create the face of the app.

Meet our Clients!

Dr. Zheng Song

Dr. Song is Robin’s primary client and faculty supervisor. He provided consistent support, guidance, and mentorship throughout the project—meeting regularly to offer technical insight, review progress, and push us to refine our work. His belief in the team’s potential helped drive Robin forward, and his continued encouragement opened doors to publishing our research at academic conferences and deploying the app in real-world environments. Robin would not have been possible without his collaboration and vision.

EIC Team

The Robin app was made possible through the ongoing support and collaboration of the EIC team, who champion accessibility and education in nature spaces.

Dr. Jacob Napieralski

Director of the Environmental Interpretive Center, Dr. Napieralski played a key role in connecting our team with the EIC and supporting our vision from the earliest stages of development.

Donna Posont

Leader of UM-Dearborn’s Birding By Ear group, Donna provided invaluable insight into the needs of visually impaired birdwatchers. Her feedback shaped Robin’s accessibility features, ensuring that the app was not only inclusive, but truly useful in real-world nature observation.

Richard Simek, Dorothy McLeer, Laura Mallard, & the EIC Staff & Fellows

We are deeply grateful to the entire EIC team for welcoming us into their space, supporting accessibility-focused innovation, and helping bring Robin to life. Their encouragement, knowledge, and willingness to integrate accessible technologies into their programs gave us the opportunity to develop, test, and refine Robin in a meaningful, real-world setting. Their collaboration was instrumental in ensuring that Robin isn't just a concept — but a working, impactful tool.
